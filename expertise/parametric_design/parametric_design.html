What are Genetic Algorithms?

Genetic algorithm is a technique for optimizing the solution to a given problem. It follows the principle of biology and uses terms and processes such as chromosomes, genomes, generation and chromosomes, mutation and section. The process of evolution starts with a generation consisting of random individuals. In each generation, the fitness of each individual is evaluated and based on their fitness value some of these individuals are selected. These individuals undergo cross over and mutation to form a new generation.

Genetic algorithms first originated from studies conducted by John Holland and his colleagues at University os Michigan on Cellular automata. Until the 1980s most of the research was theoretical until the increase in computational power which allowed for practical application. Stochastic selection is used for selection based on the fitness criteria.

GA’s are of special interest in AEC industry because of they of their ability to solve problem offering a multiplicity of a possible solution. GA’s offer bottom-up approach, as they are an emergent procedure that evolves through multiple attempt cycle.

Problems it can solve

They can resolve complex interactions between multiple factors and under multiple constraints offering solutions that occasionally surprise the designer. Some areas where we use Genetic algorithms are:-

1) Structural performance.
Structural performance is arguably the most critical performance issue that needs to be carefully studied and designed in architectural design. One can optimize structure in various ways ranging from form-finding to trying various material and their strengths.

2) Performance of the physical environment.
This category of performance includes solar, thermal, moisture, acoustics, lighting, wind and air, energy, and many others that have an impact on the quality of the built environment, both indoor and outdoor.

In our approach through generative design, we various iterations are run to check the best solution by changing orientation, size, window placement and size of the opening, overall form, etc.

In a world of green, sustainable, and low-carbon design, these performance issues are becoming the new focuses for architects to design responsibly. Different kinds of green building standards reflect his mindset and contribute to making the performance of the physical environment a more important design consideration.

3) Aesthetic and cultural performance.
These performance issues have long been the focal point in architectural design. The form, organization of space, material selection, color, shape, and details all play a role in determining the aesthetic and cultural performances of a building. These performances, contrary to the performance of the physical environment, are often difficult to quantify.

Hence, we have our own set of around hundreds of algorithms that help us quantify these intangibles.
